Timeline: Wars & Rapid Technological Acceleration

350 BC
Ancient sponge divers

Early breath-hold diving in the Mediterranean.

4th c. BC
Diving bell described by Aristotle

First concept of trapped air underwater.

1620
Early sealed-hull submersible ideas

Foundations of buoyancy and enclosure.

1775–1776
American Revolutionary War — Bushnell’s Turtle

First combat submarine attempt.

  • Ballast control
  • Manual propulsion
1861–1865
American Civil War

Submersibles and naval mine warfare.

1914–1918
World War I

Industrial submarine warfare and early detection systems.

1939–1945
World War II

Major advances in sonar, batteries, and navigation.

1953–1960
Cold War — Bathyscaphe Trieste

Human access to the deepest ocean.

1964
DSV Alvin enters service

Modern deep-sea scientific exploration begins.

1990
Shinkai 6500

Advanced maneuverable deep research submersible.

2018
DSV Limiting Factor

Reusable full-ocean-depth capability.

Today
AUVs, ROVs & AI-assisted mapping

Large-scale, autonomous ocean exploration.
