Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUV)

AUVs are untethered robots that follow pre-programmed flight paths to map the seafloor, measure water properties, or hunt for mines without human intervention.

How it Works

Unlike ROVs, AUVs have no cable connecting them to the ship. They must carry their own power and "think" for themselves using onboard computers.

Navigation (The "Blind" Problem)

GPS does not work underwater. To know where they are, AUVs use a technique called Dead Reckoning:

  • INS (Inertial Navigation System): Uses gyroscopes and accelerometers to calculate movement from a starting point.
  • DVL (Doppler Velocity Log): Sonar that pings the seafloor to measure exact speed and correct the INS drift.
  • USBL (Ultra-Short Baseline): Acoustic "pings" from the mothership to fix the AUV's position occasionally.

Communication

Radio waves cannot penetrate water. AUVs communicate via Acoustic Modems (sound waves), sending small packets of data like "Mission OK" or "Battery Low" back to the surface.
