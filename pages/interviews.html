Geopolitics

Underwater exploration is no longer only about science: it has become a strategic field where states and industries compete for knowledge, resources, and influence. High-resolution seabed mapping, access to extreme depths, and advanced robotics can translate into a long-term advantage: they support claims and negotiations, secure critical supply chains (metals needed for technology and energy transition), and shape the rules for what can be done in international waters. At the same time, this competition raises major questions about governance, environmental protection, and who controls data about the deep ocean.
