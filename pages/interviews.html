What these videos show — the major current stakes

1) Mariana Trench — competition for access, influence, and capability

  • Strategic presence: deep-ocean access is linked to influence over seabed areas and future resource options.
  • Technology race: submersibles, autonomous systems, mapping, and communications become “power enablers”.
  • Control of knowledge: owning high-resolution seabed data can shape negotiations and long-term claims.

2) Dr. Katy Croff Bell — exploration science, discovery, and why it matters

  • Scientific unknowns: deep oceans remain under-mapped; exploration drives new discoveries (ecosystems, geology).
  • Robotics & sensing: ROV/AUV, imaging, and sampling enable access to extreme environments.
  • Policy implications: better science informs protection strategies and the “rules” for future activities at depth.

3) Deep-sea mining — critical minerals, supply chains, and global governance

  • Resource security: demand for nickel/cobalt/copper drives interest in seabed minerals (and tensions).
  • Environmental risk: uncertainty about ecosystem impacts fuels calls for regulation, caution, or moratoria.
  • International rules: governance (permits, standards, monitoring) becomes a geopolitical battleground.
