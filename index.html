Key Initiatives

Two major international programmes turning ocean science into real-world impact — from mapping the seafloor to coordinating global action.

Global Mapping

Seabed 2030

Seabed 2030 is a global collaboration that aims to compile and make publicly available a complete map of the ocean floor by 2030. Reliable bathymetry is essential for safer navigation, tsunami and storm-surge modelling, climate studies, habitat mapping, and better protection of marine areas.

Underwater robotics is a key enabler: AUVs and ROVs collect high-resolution seafloor data in deep and hard-to-reach zones, improving coverage while reducing time and cost compared to traditional surveys.

UN Programme

UN Ocean Decade

The UN Ocean Decade (2021–2030) mobilises scientists, governments, NGOs and industry to deliver “the science we need for the ocean we want”. It connects research to concrete solutions: climate resilience, healthy ecosystems, sustainable food, pollution reduction, and inclusive ocean governance.

Autonomous systems are central to this effort: long-endurance drones and underwater robots expand observation networks, improve data collection at scale, and support missions in extreme environments.

About Us

We are engineering students at SeaTech Toulon, the engineering school of the University of Toulon. This website was developed as part of an English project focusing on ocean exploration, with a particular emphasis on underwater robots and autonomous systems.

Through articles, interviews and technical content, we aim to explain how modern technologies such as AUVs, ROVs and crewed submersibles contribute to scientific discovery and help us better understand the deep ocean.

Project team: Julien Gaillard, Thibaut Dani, Georges Nutall and Walid Merhazi.
